Minor Mishap's Winter Solstice Lantern Parade!

Minor Mishap's Winter Solstice Lantern Parade!
Saturday, December 21st gather 5:30pm, Parade 6-7pm
Parade starts at "The Treehouse" in Pease Park
Free Show! | Family Friendly! | Outdoors!
This is an outdoor, mobile procession, on gravel paths
Please no fire or littering

Please join us on the longest night of the year to celebrate community, light, and music with Minor Mishap's Winter Solstice Lantern Parade!

Parking Recommendation: parking near Pease Park is quite limited. If you are able to walk a bit, we recommend parking at 1301 1/2 Shoal Creek Blvd, Austin, TX 78701. There's a nice trail that goes to Pease Park that allows you to walk UNDER Lamar.

We encourage you to make flame-free lanterns in the days leading up to the event! (It's a good crafting project to do with the kiddos!) Gather your lights, lanterns, and bairn and meet us in Pease Park. We will start at 6 pm, This is a litter-free, trash-free, and flame-free event. Please take your beautiful lanterns home with you! The Winter Solstice Lantern Parade is a free and family-friendly community event! All are welcome.

The parade lasts about an hour, and the route is quite short! This is to ensure it is accessible to all, and doesn't wear out the littles too much. The plan (some chaos to be expected, so small changes could happen):

Minor Mishap Marching Band is a 25-piece renegade brass band playing original tunes inspired by everything from pickled beets to disco.

Amal Giant Puppet

October 20, 2023 @ 5:30 pm – 7:00 pm
Amal Giant Puppet
with Glass Half Full Theater Company
Congress Bridge to Capitol Grounds, Austin, TX 78701

Little Amal is a 12-foot puppet of a 10-year-old Syrian refugee girl who has traveled across 15 countries meeting more than a million people. She will journey 6,000 miles across the United States in one of the largest free public festivals ever created.

Everyone is welcome to walk with Amal.

When Amal unwittingly stumbles upon the cloud of bats that live and migrate through Austin's community. She discovers that even in the dark, you can find your way...

Join Glass Half Full Theatre, American Artists Project, Cave Lady Studios and Creative Action to welcome Amal to our city with a message of hope from Austin's local leaders, musicians and hundreds of bat puppets created by artists of all ages.

It is always free to walk with Amal. To help make a difference The Amal Fund with Choose Love supports displaced children across the world. If you can, please make a donation today: chooselove.org/theamalfund
